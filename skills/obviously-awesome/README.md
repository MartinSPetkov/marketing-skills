# Obviously Awesome — Positioning Skills Pack

A 7-skill pack built on April Dunford's *Obviously Awesome* (2019) — the most rigorous step-by-step positioning methodology for B2B tech companies.

Where most positioning frameworks tell you what to do, this pack shows you how to do it. Every skill is grounded in Dunford's 10-step process, her three positioning styles, and two decades of repositioning real products in market.

## The logic of the process

Dunford's core insight: most companies have weak positioning not because they lack good products, but because they never deliberately chose a position. They default to the market they set out to build for — even after the product evolved and the market shifted.

The 10-step process fixes this by working through positioning components in the right order:

1. Start with **best-fit customers** (not the product, not investors)
2. Look at what those customers see as **competitive alternatives**
3. List the **unique attributes** you have that alternatives don't
4. Map those to **value themes** — what can customers do because of them?
5. Ask **who cares a lot** — which customer characteristics predict high affinity?
6. Choose a **market frame of reference** that puts your strengths at the center
7. Layer on a **relevant trend** if one reinforces the positioning
8. **Capture** it so the whole company can use it

## How to use

Use skills independently or in sequence. The `positioning-workshop` skill orchestrates the full process and calls out when to run others.

Examples:
- "Walk me through the Dunford positioning process for our product" → `positioning-workshop`
- "Who would our customers use instead of us if we didn't exist?" → `competitive-alternatives`
- "Map our features to customer value" → `unique-value-mapping`
- "Should we position Head to Head against Salesforce or carve out a niche?" → `market-frame`
- "Document our positioning so the sales team can use it" → `positioning-canvas`
- "Build a sales story from our positioning" → `sales-story-builder`
- "Our market is shifting — should we update our positioning?" → `positioning-audit`

## Refusal behavior

These skills refuse to advance without the right inputs. Specifically:
- No positioning work without a named product and at least a few real customers who love it
- No competitive alternatives without grounding in customer perspective (not founder perspective)
- No market frame selection without completed value themes
- No sales story without a completed positioning canvas
- No positioning audit without current documented positioning to evaluate against

## Key distinctions from other positioning tools

This pack differs from `messaging-positioning` in a specific way: it does not start from assumptions. It starts from best-fit customers and works backward. The output is not a messaging hierarchy — it is a positioning decision that then informs messaging, pricing, product roadmap, and sales strategy simultaneously.
