# Sample Prompts — Market Frame Selection

---

## 1. Choosing between Head to Head and Big Fish Small Pond

> We have a compliance management tool with one standout capability: it integrates directly with Workday and automatically surfaces employees whose certifications are expiring before HR has to check. Most compliance tools require manual tracking. The main category is 'compliance management software' and the leader is ServiceNow. We're a 30-person startup with $3M ARR. What market frame and positioning style should we use?

**Why this works:** Clear product, clear capability, clear competitive situation (startup vs. large incumbent). The skill should recommend Big Fish, Small Pond and help identify the right subsegment (likely: companies using Workday, mid-market, or industries with heavy certification requirements like healthcare or financial services).

---

## 2. Choosing a trend to layer on

> We sell document management software for legal teams at mid-size law firms. We're in the 'legal document management' category and doing well. Lately, every conversation with new buyers turns to AI — they all want to know how AI will help them. We have AI-powered clause extraction and contract comparison features. How do we use this trend in our positioning without over-hyping it?

**Why this works:** Clear request for trend layering guidance with a specific concern about over-reliance on hype. The skill will evaluate whether AI fits the three-circle intersection (product strength, market context, trend).

---

## 3. Evaluating Create a New Game

> Our product combines real-time location data, demand forecasting, and autonomous pricing in one platform for parking operators. No existing category really captures what we do — 'parking management software' doesn't communicate the AI pricing angle, and 'revenue management software' doesn't communicate the real-time operational angle. Should we try to create a new category?

**Why this works:** Genuine candidate for Create a New Game evaluation. The skill will check the conditions: is no existing category a good fit? Is there an enabling shift (AI/location data)? Does the company have resources for a category-creation effort?

---

## Tips

**Share your current category.** The skill needs to know what you currently call yourself to evaluate whether the category is helping or hurting.

**Describe the competitive situation honestly.** Are you the leader? A challenger? A niche player? The positioning style depends entirely on this reality.

**Tell us what the leader looks like.** If there is an established leader in your market, name them and briefly describe what they do well that you can't match. That gap defines whether Big Fish Small Pond is viable.

**Give the trend context.** If you want help with trend layering, name the trend you're considering and describe whether your customers already use that language. Trends work when buyers already care — they don't create demand out of nothing.
