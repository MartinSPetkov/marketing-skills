# Sample Prompts — Competitive Alternatives

---

## 1. Identifying alternatives from customer behavior

> We sell a B2B invoicing tool for freelancers. Our best customers are designers and developers who invoice clients weekly. When we onboard them, they always say things like "I was just sending PDF invoices over email," "I used to use a Google Docs template," or "I just had a spreadsheet I updated by hand." We also see Salt competitors in deals sometimes but rarely. Help me build our competitive alternatives list.

**Why this works:** Provides best-fit customers (freelancers, high invoice frequency) and includes direct customer quotes about what they used before. The skill can immediately identify manual processes as the primary alternative.

---

## 2. Founder vs. customer gap scenario

> Our sales team has built a whole battlecard against Competitor X because that's who we keep hearing about in deals. But honestly, most of our actual wins come from customers who were "managing this in Notion" or "using a shared Google Sheet." Are those really our competitors? It feels embarrassing to say we compete with Google Sheets.

**Why this works:** This is the classic gap between founder-facing competitors and customer-facing alternatives. The skill will address why Google Sheets is a legitimate and important primary alternative to document.

---

## 3. New category with "do nothing" as primary

> We built a tool that helps e-commerce brands detect when a competitor changes their price and automatically adjusts their pricing in response. Our best customers are brands that were "just checking competitor sites manually once a week" or "not monitoring at all." Almost nobody we talk to has evaluated other automated repricing tools. What are our competitive alternatives?

**Why this works:** Classic nascent category where "do nothing" and manual processes dominate. The skill will put "do nothing" as a primary cluster and explain what that means for positioning.

---

## Tips

**Ground every answer in the best-fit customer list.** The skill needs to know who is doing the comparing, not just what the alternatives are in the abstract.

**Share what customers said before they switched to you.** The "what were you using before" question is the most direct path to the true primary alternative.

**Be honest about losing to 'do nothing.'** Many teams are embarrassed to list "they did nothing" as a competitor. They should not be. It is often the primary alternative for early-market products and profoundly shapes the value story.

**Separate investor competitors from customer competitors.** The startups you follow on Twitter are investor competitors. What matters for positioning is what customers actually compare you to. These lists are usually very different.
