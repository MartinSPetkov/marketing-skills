# Sample Prompts — Positioning Canvas

---

## 1. Building the canvas from completed components

> We've just finished the Dunford positioning process. Here's what we've got:
> - Product: OTTO Motors autonomous delivery vehicles
> - Market category: Autonomous industrial vehicles
> - Primary competitive alternative: Manual forklift operators and fixed conveyor systems
> - Secondary: Traditional manufacturing robots (stationary)
> - Unique attributes: maps and navigates facility dynamically, fleet dispatch and management, vehicle-style headlights/taillights (visual signaling), standard vehicle interface for operators
> - Value: materials move continuously and adapt to changing production needs without rerouting fixed infrastructure; fleet-level visibility into delivery status; operators familiar with vehicle controls, not robotics programming
> - Who cares a lot: manufacturers with variable production flows, facilities doing frequent layout changes, warehouses with high labor costs for manual movement
> - Trend: rise of autonomous vehicles — buyers already familiar with the concept from consumer context
>
> Build the canvas.

**Why this works:** All five components are provided. The skill can assemble and quality-check immediately.

---

## 2. Building the canvas with gaps to surface

> Here's our positioning to document. Product is a marketing automation tool. We compete with Mailchimp and manual email campaigns. Our differentiator is that we track not just email opens but full website behavior and score leads based on buying intent. Market: marketing automation. Who cares: B2B marketers who need to identify which leads are sales-ready. Can you build the canvas?

**Why this works:** Some gaps are present (value is implicit but not stated, 'who cares' is underdeveloped). The skill will build what it can and flag what needs work.

---

## 3. Converting a positioning statement to a canvas

> We have this positioning statement we've been using: 'For enterprise security teams who need to respond to threats in real time, our SIEM platform is the only solution that combines AI-powered detection with automated playbooks, unlike legacy SIEM tools that require manual investigation.' We want to turn this into a proper Dunford canvas because the statement is too awkward to use internally. Can you help?

**Why this works:** The positioning statement contains most of the components in compressed form. The skill will extract and expand them into the canvas format.

---

## Tips

**Provide all components before asking for the canvas.** The canvas assembles the work done in previous steps — it does not generate positioning from scratch. If you haven't worked through Steps 4–9, do that first.

**Tell us which parts you're uncertain about.** If you're not confident in your competitive alternatives or your 'who cares' definition, say so. The skill will flag those cells in the canvas for validation rather than treating them as confirmed.

**Share the intended audience.** A canvas for an internal all-hands meeting needs different framing than one for the sales team's reference guide or the product team's roadmap planning session.
