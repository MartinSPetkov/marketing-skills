# Product Go-to-Market Canvas

From Chapter 19 of *Loved* by Martina Lauchengco. A working document that maps a year of GTM activity across four swim lanes, anchored quarterly.

## How to use this template

Fill it in top-down, left-to-right. The quarters across the top are your time horizon. The four rows are the swim lanes. Each cell holds 1 to 5 bullets, no paragraphs.

The canvas is meant to be co-owned by PMM, product, marketing, and sales leadership. If only PMM can read it, it isn't working. Share it in its draft form. Revise it with each function. The point is the alignment conversation, not the artifact.

## The canvas

## What goes in each swim lane

**Customer / outside environment.** What's changing in your buyer's world this quarter. Competitors launching. Regulation shifting. Industry events happening. Macro shifts (recession, AI reset, compliance deadline). Not everything happening in the world. Things that change how your customer buys.

**Product milestones.** What ships. What reaches GA. What hits a usage or revenue milestone. What's behind or ahead of its promise. Do not include every engineering backlog item. Include the things a buyer, a sales rep, or an analyst would want to know about.

**Marketing strategies.** The big themes and plays for the quarter. A launch. A vertical push. A competitive displacement campaign. A conference presence. An analyst push. A shift in positioning. The strategies should tie back to a GTM goal, not a marketing channel goal.

**Key activities.** The specific, time-bound work. Who owns it. When it ships. Think Asana board, not vision doc. This is the row where execution lives.

## The six-step build (Ch 19)

1. **Start with the customer.** Fill the customer / outside environment row first. If you start with the product, you end up with feature marketing.
2. **Layer in product milestones.** Only the milestones that matter to a buyer.
3. **Sketch marketing strategies.** Two to four per quarter. Over-filling this row is a red flag. You're not going to run eight strategies in a quarter.
4. **Tie each strategy to a customer or product driver.** If a strategy doesn't trace back to something in rows 1 or 2, ask why it exists.
5. **Add activities.** The execution row. Assign owners and dates.
6. **Review cross-functionally.** Walk it with product, marketing, sales. Update. Ship.

## Common failure modes

**Product milestones dominating the page.** If rows 2 and 4 are full and row 1 is empty, you're planning around your roadmap, not around the market. Rebuild starting from the customer row.

**Marketing strategies untied to customer drivers.** A strategy with no cell in row 1 to explain it is a vanity campaign. Cut it or anchor it.

**Company-first language throughout.** "We launch" / "We announce" / "We release." Replace with "Customers discover" / "Buyers evaluate" / "Champions adopt." The canvas shouldn't read like a company press calendar.

**Treating it as a slide instead of a working doc.** The canvas lives in a place that can be updated. Confluence, Notion, a shared doc. Not a deck PMM updates once a quarter.

## When to revisit

At least once a quarter. Realistically: any time a row-1 event lands that wasn't planned for. A competitor launches something unexpected. A customer segment shifts. A product slip pushes a milestone two quarters out. Update the canvas same week.
