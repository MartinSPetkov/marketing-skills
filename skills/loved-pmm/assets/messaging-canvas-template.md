# Messaging Canvas

From Chapter 25 of *Loved*. A single page that holds positioning, segments, pillars, value areas, and evidence. When it's done, the messaging is done. Everything downstream (home page, sales deck, ads) is an expression of what's on this canvas.

## The seven-step build (Ch 25)

1. **Name the segments.** Primary, secondary, explicitly deprioritized. If you can't name who you're ignoring, your messaging will try to serve everyone and serve no one.
2. **Write draft positioning.** Use the formula as input. "For [target] who [need], [product] is a [category] that [benefit]; unlike [alternative], our product [differentiator]." Don't publish this sentence. Turn it into plain language.
3. **Identify pillars.** Three to five themes you'll always come back to. A pillar is not a feature. It's the thing a buyer would remember after a 30-minute demo.
4. **Translate each pillar to value.** What does the buyer get? Time saved, risk reduced, revenue earned, headcount avoided. Be specific. "Better collaboration" is not a value statement.
5. **Gather evidence.** For each pillar, 2-3 pieces of proof. Customer quotes. Metrics. Third-party recognition. A pillar without evidence is an assertion.
6. **Apply the CAST filter.** Clear, Authentic, Simple, Testable. Rewrite anything that fails.
7. **Test in the real medium.** A home page headline. A cold email subject line. A landing-page hero. Don't test in a doc. Medium reveals jargon.

## CAST filter

**Clear.** Would a smart customer understand it on first read without asking a clarifying question?

**Authentic.** Does it sound like your company? Would a sales rep say it out loud without cringing?

**Testable.** Can you put it next to a competitor's message and have customers tell which is which? If your message could belong to three other companies, it isn't differentiated.

**Simple.** No jargon. No throat-clearing. No synonym stacking. Every word earns its place.

## Common failure modes

**Formula as published copy.** The formula sentence appears verbatim on the home page. Fix: the formula is input, the home page is output. They should not be the same text.

**Too many pillars.** Six, eight, ten. Nobody will remember more than four. Cut.

**Evidence row empty.** You're shipping aspiration. Either get evidence or rewrite the pillar.

**Company POV throughout.** "Our platform empowers..." "We deliver..." "Our comprehensive solution..." Rewrite from customer POV: "Teams get..." "Buyers can..." "Developers ship..."

**Positioning sentence that could describe a competitor.** If you can swap your name for a competitor's and the sentence still reads true, the differentiator isn't doing its job.

## When to revisit

Full rebuild: once a year, or when the company pivots (new segment, new product line, new category position).

Light refresh: each quarter, pass the canvas against the evidence row and update proof points with fresh customer quotes and metrics. Stale evidence erodes trust faster than stale pillars do.
