# PMM Metrics

Chapter 13. The shortest chapter for a reason: Lauchengco is arguing against over-measuring PMM with the wrong yardsticks.

## The core caveat

> Product marketing doesn't have many hard metrics showing a job well done. There are some shorter-term indicators, but for most product marketing outcomes, they take time to know if they worked.

This sentence is the whole chapter. If a leader wants a weekly PMM dashboard that looks like a demand-gen dashboard, the leader is about to measure the wrong things and punish the PMM for the wrong misses.

## The division of labor

Sales and marketing metrics focus on shorter-term business need: top-of-funnel volume, pipeline, revenue. PMM metrics focus on what matters for the longer-term future of the business: category position, competitive win rate, evangelism, CAC trend, retention, NPS as a leading indicator.

If someone hands a PMM an OKR with MQLs, SQLs, and pipeline in it, that's a marketing OKR wearing a PMM name tag. The PMM should still attend to those numbers because they indicate whether the GTM engine is working, but owning them means owning the wrong thing.

## Example PMM OKRs (Ch 13)

Five worth remembering, because they pass the "is this PMM or is this marketing?" test:

- Become recognized category leader by major analyst firm(s).
- Launch product to improve market awareness indicators by 10% and enable adoption by [key new market] as % of customer base.
- Position product as redefining [concept] as evidenced by inbound or category mentions of [concept].
- Enable sales to win >50% of competitive deals.
- Grow organic evangelism by 10% on key digital and social platforms.

Notice what they have in common: each is a perception or position or competitive outcome, not a funnel volume. Each takes a quarter or more to move. Each has a clear customer or market mechanism behind it.

## Metrics a PMM watches, by lens

This is not the PMM's dashboard. It's the full set of metrics a PMM pays attention to so they can spot market signal inside someone else's numbers.

### Product lens

- HEART metrics if the product team uses them: Happiness, Engagement, Acquisition, Retention, Task Success. PMM brings relevant learnings from product into marketing, not the other way around.
- Customer funnel metrics. PMM adjusts tactics per stage.

### Marketing lens

- Customer journey engagement.
- MQLs, or PQLs if the motion is product-led.
- Inbound discovery: organic search plus direct search as a percentage of visitors, and how that shifts over time. This is a slow but honest signal.

### Sales lens

- Sales cycle time.
- Win rates: closed-won vs. lost, and split by competitor.

When win rate drops, the PMM is the diagnostician. Lauchengco names the full examination: training, tools, process, playbook, marketing mix, messaging, positioning, pricing, packaging, third-party evangelism. "The full product marketing stack." If you're only looking at one or two, you'll attribute the loss wrong.

### Financial lens

- Conversion rates by product.
- CAC. Should trend down or stay level. Rising CAC is often a messaging or positioning problem masquerading as a channel problem.
- LTV.
- Retention (lagging). NPS as a leading indicator.

## Patience and persistence

Lauchengco's anchor quote for this chapter:

> Good product marketing takes time, and measuring its effectiveness does too.

The practical implication for a PMM leader: get clarity up front from the CEO and the head of marketing on what they expect from the role. Write it down. Then use that written charter to respond to market realities when a short-term miss shows up. Without the charter, every missed MQL target turns into a referendum on PMM.

## Red flags in a PMM scorecard

A scorecard that is all short-term marketing metrics. PMM is being measured as marketing with extra steps.

A scorecard with no competitive win rate. The company doesn't know if its positioning works against the alternatives customers actually consider.

A scorecard with no category or analyst indicator. The company has given up on being positioned in the market and is just chasing leads.

A scorecard with NPS tracked but not fed back into messaging or positioning work. The signal is being collected and ignored.

## What to do with this reference

When the user asks you to set PMM OKRs or a PMM scorecard, do three things. Name the business outcome the PMM can plausibly move in a quarter. Pick two or three metrics from the lenses above that show movement toward it. State one longer-horizon indicator that the quarter is building toward (analyst mention, win rate trend, NPS). Then tell them what they should not put on the scorecard and why.
