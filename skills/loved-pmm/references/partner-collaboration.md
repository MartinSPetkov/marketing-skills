# Partnering with Product, Marketing, and Sales

Three chapters in the book (8, 9, 10) cover how PMM works with its three partner functions. Same structure each time: setup, indicators of a working partnership, anti-patterns with what better looks like, cadence. This file consolidates all three.

## Partnering with Product Management (Ch 8)

"Product marketing is the go-to-market yin to product management's product yang."

### Ratio

PM:PMM ratios range from 1:1 to 1:5, averaging 1:2.5. Forrester's 2022 benchmark cited 2.6 PMs per 1 PMM. Lean PMM orgs run ratios that high. Companies trying to run effective PGTMs at 1:8 or 1:10 are starving the function.

### How to align PMMs to PMs

Two factors: how customers experience the product, and where the business wants to grow. Five common alignment patterns by stage:

1. **Startup.** PMM embedded with product and GTM; rapid discovery and iteration.
2. **Individual product forward.** Designated PMM-to-PM relationship. Each product has a story positioning its strengths.
3. **Product suites forward.** PMMs shift to suite level and customer segments. Large companies may have PMMs at product, suite, and customer-segment level at once.
4. **Verticals or new markets.** PMMs focused on verticals, may not have PM counterparts.
5. **Customer segments.** Market-needs-driven, not product-driven. Looking for market wedges.

### Indicators the partnership is working

- The why behind the PGTM is understood. PMs feel it matches the product.
- Product squads want PMM in major product decisions.
- PMs and PMMs collaborate heavily on positioning and messaging. PMs are comfortable with technical accuracy and alignment with vision.
- PMs are highly engaged in analyst relations.
- Competitive response is rapid, collaborative, coordinated.
- Pricing and packaging ownership is clear.
- PMs can offload collateral, content, sales enablement with minimal consultation.

### Anti-patterns, what better looks like

**"What product ships, sales can't sell."** Tech-first vacuum. Engineers build, sales can't explain it, customers don't buy. Better: PMM is a partner in product planning, empowered to challenge plans out of line with market realities.

**Product ships at a terrible time to market it.** Announced the week of major industry events, holidays, Apple keynotes. Better: PMM represents market and customer perspective before timing decisions are locked.

**PMs pulled into collateral or sales support constantly.** Usually because PMM doesn't know the product well enough to speak for it. Better: PM invests upfront in getting PMM up to speed.

### Cadence

- Ongoing: market-fit conversation.
- Weekly (minimum): PMM in product squad meeting.
- Bi-monthly or monthly: product planning review.
- Quarterly: PGTM + product planning review using the PGTM Canvas.

## Partnering with Marketing (Ch 9)

PMM is "the product's ambassador to the marketing team." In most orgs, PMM reports into marketing.

### Workiva anchor story

Workiva held a multi-day event for SEC financial professionals. Spin-to-Win, make-a-sundae mason jars, aquarium socials, theme-park evenings. Attendees became raving fans in a community "not really known for that." The event paid for itself every year.

### The marketing mix — what connects to what objective (Ch 9)

Different objectives call for different mixes. This map is the PMM's working reference when a campaign is being planned:

- **Making customers aware of problem, solution, company.** Advertising (traditional and digital), website, SEO, press articles, analyst reports.
- **Encouraging consideration.** White papers, videos, customer stories, events, email, ABM, direct mail, partnerships, PR, press articles.
- **Encouraging purchase or renewal.** Pricing, packaging, customer events, reviews. Customer success plays a big role here.
- **Enhancing awareness and loyalty around the brand.** Customer community, social, content, newsletters, influencers, third-party events, press articles.

"A broad, well-coordinated mix, not any one thing, is the winning formula."

### Indicators the partnership is working

Marketing understands market nuances and segmentation. Marketing understands why the product is valued, not just which features it has. Marketing understands the why behind recommended activities. Product naming decisions happen collaboratively with the company brand in mind. CAC stays sustainable. Teams collaborate on adapting when the market shifts.

### Setup

In most orgs, PMM reports under marketing. Agile marketing led by a PMM with weekly scrums is the recommended operating rhythm (see `references/release-scale.md` for the agile marketing principles).

### Anti-patterns

**Campaigns perform but product isn't getting positioned.** Easy to optimize a campaign metric without laddering up to a business need. Better: PMM is part of campaign planning.

**"Future state" messaging too far ahead of current reality.** Aspiration without product to back it. Detracts from credibility. Better: PMM finds the line between aspirational and current and defends it.

**Creative that pushes the edge but doesn't connect.** Wins awards, loses customers. Better: test ideas with customers before deciding.

### Cadence

Weekly alignment meetings. Monthly activity reviews and funnel metrics. Quarterly PGTM and funnel revisit.

## Partnering with Sales (Ch 10)

### The natural tension

"Sales wants to get it done now. Product marketing wants to get it done right."

Both are right. The job is managing the tension, not eliminating it.

### The paper datasheets anchor

A mid-market sales team missed quota and demanded paper datasheets as their only ask. The actual problem: they were going after non-target customers. "It was that there was a demand, not a discussion, in which sales presumed the solution (paper data sheet) instead of asking for what they needed: a more memorable prompt encouraging follow-up."

Lesson: when sales asks for a specific deliverable, ask what problem it's solving. The requested artifact is often a symptom.

### Most powerful tools for a sales partnership

Two, in order:

1. **Reference customers.** Prerequisite for everything else.
2. **A sales playbook.** Shows appropriate actions by stage, next step, relevant tools, and what criteria qualifies a customer to flow from one stage to the next. A good playbook can make an average rep successful faster than average.

Reference customers without a playbook: underused social proof. Playbook without reference customers: fiction.

### Prioritization principle

"When a direct salesforce is involved, prioritize adjustments from the bottom of the customer funnel before moving up to the top stages." Close-the-deal problems compound faster than top-of-funnel problems. Fix bottom first, then open the top.

### Key joint activities (Ch 10)

Do not split this list between sales and marketing. These are co-owned.

- Jointly defined ICPs and target account lists.
- Customer journey maps.
- Sales presentations, call scripts, email templates.
- Product datasheets, videos, website content.
- Demos grounded in key messages (not feature walks).
- Competitive response tools.
- Usable customer stories and case studies.
- Customer advisory board.
- Identification of new target markets.
- Key events.
- Sales training and on-target accountability.

### Indicators the partnership is working

Sales knowledgeable and targeting the right ICP. Playbook actually followed. Healthy pipeline flow. Materials create urgency while staying appropriately aspirational. A wide array of relevant, timely, on-message marketing actions and assets available.

### Setup

PMM attends weekly pipeline reviews. Partnership evolves as sales becomes more predictable. Packaging is PMM-led. PMM has a heavy hand in pricing. Sales input on pricing is essential; sales ownership of pricing is not.

### Anti-patterns

**Marketing as a service to sales.** Activities focus on immediate not strategic. Better: examine what moves toward PGTM goals; use data to prioritize over loudest voice.

**Lack of awareness before sales engages.** "Spray and pray" costs a lot and converts little. Better: targeted and ABM approaches coordinated with the customer journey.

**Lack of adherence to playbook or official materials.** Mavericks damage positioning. Better: co-create tools with reps so they own them, then hold reps accountable.

### Memorable framing

"If product is the engine of growth, sales is the gas. Marketing is the gas station. Product marketing is the gas station attendant making sure sales gets tanked up."

Use sparingly. It's sticky but you'll burn it out if you repeat it every meeting.

### Cadence

Weekly or bi-weekly pipeline and marketing reviews. Monthly joint funnel analysis and SLA monitoring. Quarterly PGTM and funnel revisit.

## A shared observation across all three partnerships

Partnership health is a leading indicator of PGTM health. When PMM is fighting with product over technical accuracy, fighting with marketing over creative direction, or fighting with sales over what to ship this quarter, the PGTM itself is almost always the missing artifact. A filled-in PGTM Canvas (Ch 19) with shared quarterly revisits resolves most of these fights by removing the ambiguity that drives them.

When you're asked to fix a partnership, first ask whether the three functions have a shared picture of the quarter. If they don't, build one.
