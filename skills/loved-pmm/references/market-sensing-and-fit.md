# Market Sensing and Discovering Market Fit

Ambassador work in practice. Covers ongoing market sensing (Ch 3) and the harder, more diagnostic work of probing for market fit and rediscovering it as a product matures (Ch 11).

## Market fit, defined

Market fit is not product-market fit reduced to a survey question. Lauchengco's framing:

> Discovering "market pull." What makes a customer need or want your product enough that they take action to learn more, try, or buy. And what makes this pattern repeatable.

Two parts. The pull, and the repeatability. You can have a single customer pulling hard and no fit. You can have a hundred curious tire-kickers and no fit. Fit is a repeating pattern of action.

## Before market-fit questions: product baseline

PMs own these. PMMs should know them cold too.

- Are your customers who you think they are?
- Do they really have the problems you think they have?
- How does the customer solve this problem today?
- What would be required for them to switch?

## Market-fit questions (Ch 11)

Split into value and growth / connection.

### Value questions

- Who is most likely to use this? Who buys it? Who influences decisions? (Three different people most of the time.)
- Do they prioritize the problem?
- What else are they considering that is similar?
- What have I said or shown you that is most compelling? (The aha moment.)
- Relative urgency: "If you had 10 points in your budget to allocate to solve your most urgent problems, how many points would each get?" (Forces tradeoff, reveals real priority.)
- Pricing: How much would you expect to pay? Maximum willing to pay? What else have you recently bought at this price?
- What new product have you most recently bought, and why?

### Growth and connection questions

- What created curiosity?
- How would you describe this to a colleague? (Messaging gold. Capture their exact words.)
- Where would you expect to see this product talked about?
- How would you expect to assess it?
- What would make you a raving fan?
- Who are the next most likely market segments to adopt? Do they feel the same sense of urgency? What market situations are necessary for them to act?

## Discovery techniques for probing market fit

### Exit surveys

A single-question pop-up on churn or cancellation. Example: "Is there anything we could have done differently to make you stay?" One question beats ten.

### A/B messaging tests

Don't only pay attention to which variant performed better. Ask what the difference tells you about market perception. If the aspirational version underperforms the feature-led one, your buyers aren't ready for aspiration yet.

### Demand test variation

Put a click-to-buy or click-to-trial on a landing page and see who clicks. Separates curious from committed. Follow up with a "why did you decide to buy?" prompt on the confirmation page.

### Ad testing

Test vastly different directions at once, not small copy variants. Aspirational vs product-led vs problem-led. The winner reveals market readiness.

### Sentiment probe

Baseline on a 7 or 10-point scale. Show a video or demo. Re-measure. Ask those who shifted what moved them.

### Usability test variation

Include competitors' websites in the task flow. Watch how real users search for your product or your space. You learn what language they expect and where they look first.

## Creative market-test examples from the book

- **Developer app-security tool.** Signs placed at a transit stop. The more specific message got fewer glances but actual walk-ups. Specific beats catchy.
- **Wearable sleep tracker.** Survey in front of Walgreens, then repeated with a silent actor in a lab coat nodding behind the surveyor. Implied medical endorsement measurably raised perceived value.
- **Online coding bootcamps.** A value-matching game across five competitors. People paid more for the brand they recognized even when they claimed they weren't brand-driven.

## Techniques for existing products (Ch 11)

When you have customers, use the signal they already produce:

- **Win/loss analysis.** Not who you lose to — the *why* you lose. Interview won and lost deals. Patterns surface in 10 to 20 calls.
- **Sales call shadowing.** Body language and tonal "pretty good" vs "wow" don't show up in CRM text fields. Go to the calls.
- **Intent data.** Technographic + firmographic + account-action platforms (Bombora, 6sense, Demandbase, etc.).
- **Social and customer sentiment.** App reviews, G2, Glassdoor, Twitter/X, Reddit. Reviews with fewer than 5 stars often have the highest information density.

## Timeboxing

"Allocate fixed time periods (a week to a month) for a learning period, then assess." Don't wait for definitive data. Market-fit work is iterative. Pick a question, set a window, commit to the interpretation at the end.

## Active listening discipline

Lead with open-ended questions before introducing your own ideas. "If you lead with your ideas, you've primed the response." Use calibration tools to make soft answers concrete: ratings, rankings, "would you hit the like button?" Capture the customer's exact phrases, not your paraphrase.

## The Word / legal anchor story

In Lauchengco's last year on Word, WordPerfect still held a foothold in legal. Microsoft ran a national tour of law firms, analyzed hundreds of documents, did dozens of interviews. Finding: Word's formatting limitations required a layout engine rewrite that couldn't ship soon. Her response: pivot near-term focus to tech-forward law firms that wanted the whole Office suite. Market-fit probing exposed a product gap and led to a segment shift, not a product promise.

## What the PMM does with the output

1. Document repeatedly-heard phrases verbatim.
2. Feed them back into messaging (see `references/messaging-process.md`).
3. Sharpen the ICP or target persona.
4. Flag to product any repeated pattern that a roadmap shift could resolve — then let the PM decide.
5. Refresh the PGTM canvas with any implication for strategy (see `references/product-gtm-canvas.md`).

Market sensing that doesn't change anyone's behavior is performance. The test of Ambassador work is whether something downstream shifts.
