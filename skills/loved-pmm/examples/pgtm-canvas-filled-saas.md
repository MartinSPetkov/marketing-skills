# PGTM Canvas: filled example for a B2B SaaS company

A worked example from Chapter 19 of *Loved*, extrapolated to a common scenario. This shows what a Product Go-to-Market Canvas looks like when completed for a mid-stage SaaS company in a realistic year.

## The company

For this example: "Hexagon," a fictional mid-stage SaaS company. AI-powered contract review software. 200 employees. $35M ARR. Selling into legal ops and procurement teams at mid-market and enterprise customers. Currently at Rapid Rise stage (see `references/pmm-career-and-stage.md`), competing against Ironclad, LinkSquares, and Contract Logix.

## The canvas for 2026

| Swim lane | Q1 2026 | Q2 2026 | Q3 2026 | Q4 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Customer / outside environment** | New EU AI Act enforcement begins Feb 1; legal teams under pressure to audit AI use | Gartner publishes revised Magic Quadrant for Contract Lifecycle Management in May | Ironclad announces IPO roadshow; competitive attention peaks | Q4 budgets approved; procurement teams locking 2027 vendor selections |
| **Product milestones** | Audit Trail for AI Decisions (GA Feb 15) | Procurement integrations (SAP, Coupa) ship mid-May | AI risk scoring module (GA in Aug) | Enterprise-tier SSO and SOC 2 Type II automation (GA Dec) |
| **Marketing strategies** | AI governance positioning push; lead with EU AI Act readiness | Analyst engagement for Gartner MQ; category repositioning from "CLM" to "AI-aware contract ops" | Competitive displacement campaign vs Ironclad; "stable alternative" message | Enterprise-ready season; case studies from Fortune 1000 deployments |
| **Key activities** | Webinar series on EU AI Act (Jan 28, Feb 12, Feb 26); analyst briefings w/ Gartner, Forrester in Jan; refreshed homepage by Feb 10 | Gartner MQ briefing deck due Apr 1; customer reference pushes for 8 MQ references; category messaging doc finalized Apr 15 | 6-week paid media flight Aug 1 to Sep 15; sales battlecard v3 ships July 20; 3 win/loss deep dives published Aug | 4 enterprise case studies published by Oct 15; roadshow in 6 cities Oct-Nov; 2027 analyst briefing round in Dec |

## Reading the canvas

### Row 1 (customer / outside environment)

Each quarter is anchored to something in the buyer's world that Hexagon didn't manufacture. The EU AI Act is real and dated. The Gartner MQ cycle is predictable. Ironclad's IPO rumor shifts competitive dynamics. Q4 budget season is calendar-driven.

Notice what's not on this row: Hexagon's internal OKRs. Hexagon's board meetings. Hexagon's hiring plans. Those don't belong on a PGTM canvas. They go in a separate operating plan. The PGTM canvas is market-facing.

### Row 2 (product milestones)

Four milestones for the year. Each one is a thing a buyer would care about. Audit Trail for AI Decisions is a direct response to the regulatory pressure in row 1. Procurement integrations enable the new segment. AI risk scoring responds to the category trend. Enterprise SSO and SOC 2 unlocks enterprise deals.

Notice what's missing: the engineering backlog. Mobile app improvements. Billing system refactors. Performance optimizations. Those are real work but they aren't buyer-visible. They don't belong here.

### Row 3 (marketing strategies)

Each quarter has one strategy. Quarterly strategies, not monthly. Each one is tied to something in row 1 or row 2.

- Q1: EU AI Act pressure (row 1) + Audit Trail ships (row 2). Tied.
- Q2: Gartner MQ cycle (row 1) + the repositioning is not product-milestone-tied, it's strategic. That's a choice the PMM is making to use the analyst cycle as a re-brand moment.
- Q3: Ironclad IPO distraction (row 1) + AI risk scoring (row 2). Tied.
- Q4: Budget season (row 1) + enterprise-readiness milestones (row 2). Tied.

Every strategy has a clear "why now" anchored in the customer's world.

### Row 4 (key activities)

Concrete, dated, owner-assigned (owners not shown here but would be in the real doc). Activities tie back to strategies. Webinars support the EU AI Act push. Analyst briefings support the MQ cycle. Battlecards support competitive displacement. Case studies support enterprise readiness.

## What Hexagon is explicitly not doing

A good PGTM canvas makes saying-no visible. For Hexagon, the canvas implies they are not:

- Running a generalized brand campaign in 2026.
- Expanding internationally this year (no row-1 or row-3 activity points to it).
- Building out a partner program (not on the canvas, so not a priority).
- Entering adjacent categories (no product milestones suggest it).

All of those might be good ideas. But a canvas that tries to hold every possible priority is just a wish list. Hexagon's PMM made choices.

## Common failure modes this canvas avoids

**Row 1 empty.** Canvas is built from the product out. Fix: start from the customer.

**Row 3 overloaded.** Five strategies per quarter. Fix: cut to one or two.

**Row 4 untied to row 3.** Activities exist because a team wanted to do them, not because they serve a strategy. Fix: trace each activity to a strategy to a customer or product driver.

**Milestones in row 2 that no buyer cares about.** Fix: ask, "would a buyer evaluating us notice this shipped?" If no, drop it from row 2.

## How this canvas gets used

Monthly review with PMM, PM, marketing, sales leadership. Look at the current quarter's row and ask: is the customer-row assumption holding? Have any products slipped? Are strategies on track? Are activities shipping?

Quarterly review: redraw the next quarter in detail, revise the subsequent quarters, and add the next quarter out.

Annual planning: a full rebuild.

## When to extrapolate from this example

When a user has a similar profile (mid-stage B2B SaaS, named competitors, identifiable buyer personas, a known analyst cycle in their category), this template generalizes. Walk through the four rows with them, asking the questions above in order.

When the user is earlier stage (pre-product-market fit or in Ignition), the canvas is less useful. At that stage, market signal is still being discovered. A PGTM canvas that locks in a year of assumptions will be wrong by Q2. Better: use the market sensing work in `references/market-sensing-and-fit.md` and revisit the canvas when GTM feels repeatable.

When the user is at a truly massive scale (F500 divisions of divisions), the canvas still works but you'll need one per business unit. The same four swim lanes, per unit, per year.
